<p><p>The gear and transmission lubricants market is primarily divided into two types: synthetic oil and mineral oil. Synthetic oils are artificially engineered for superior performance, offering enhanced protection, improved thermal stability, and better resistance to oxidation. They typically provide longer service intervals and efficiency in extreme temperatures. In contrast, mineral oils are derived from refining crude oil, making them more cost-effective but with limited performance in extreme conditions. Both types cater to various applications depending on performance requirements and budget considerations.</p></p>
