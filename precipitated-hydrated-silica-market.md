Granular silica has a larger particle size, offering advantages in applications like rubber and plastics, where enhanced flow and reduced dust are essential. Powdered silica, on the other hand, features finer particles, making it ideal for applications that require high surface area, such as cosmetics, food additives, and pharmaceuticals.
